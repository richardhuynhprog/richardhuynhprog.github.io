Education

I went to F. W. Begley Elementary School in Windsor, Ontario, Canada. I grew up living close to this school so I would walk to class and back. I was impressed by the teaching standards set by Canadians. I learned more than academics from the teachers - they left an impression on my values and on how to be a member of society.

I moved in seventh grade to Southfield, Michigan, USA. My middle school was Glenn W. Levey Middle School, and my high school was Southfield High School. It was almost three kilometers away, but I would take the bus there and back. It was very new in terms of technology, and I picked up how to wrestle folkstyle from the high school team.

Hometown

Southfield, Michigan was a predominantly Jewish community before they moved away in “white flight” of the 1950’s. The previous owners left but the community stayed, and Southfield built public spaces and libraries for its people. The city had less of a downtown scene and focused on attracting businesses and families.

Unregulated business practices in the surrounding area led to the pollution of the local river Rouge, which stretched across multiple counties. The water was extremely contaminated with pollutants which had industrial waste dumped into them and years after the Clean Water Act of 1972 it wasn’t safe to swim in growing up. I remember cleaning the River Rouge with friends and volunteers while I lived there and recently the EPA has involved itself in the clean up.

Family

My father is Hung Huynh, a Vietnamese immigrant who was granted Canadian citizenship in the late 1980s. Leaving communism behind him, Hung participates regularly at every election and exercises his right to vote. My mother is Tham Huynh, an Asian-American immigrant who started her own business when I was a small child and loved to plant flowers. I have one sibling, an older brother named Robert. He is married and has a job.

The last family member is Theodora the Golden Chow-Chow. She is very skittish of adult humans and does not like people unless they are children. She does not play fetch but is an active dog that prefers to have multiple walks a day or else she’ll try to escape and walk herself before coming home on her own.
